DATA EXPLANATION
================
Gemini BTC Prediction Market Data
Collected: 2026-03-03
Source: Gemini Exchange Public API (api.gemini.com)


OVERVIEW
--------
This dataset contains historical market data for Gemini's BTC prediction markets.
These markets are binary options on the spot price of Bitcoin. Each contract
resolves to $1.00 if Bitcoin's price is above a specified strike price at a
specified expiry time, and $0.00 if it is not. During trading, contracts trade
at prices between $0.01 and $0.99, which can be interpreted as the market's
implied probability that Bitcoin will be above that strike at expiry.

Example: A contract priced at 0.72 implies a 72% probability that BTC will
be above its strike price at expiry.


MARKET STRUCTURE
----------------
Each "event" represents a specific expiry moment (e.g., "BTC price on March 3
at 7pm EST"). Within each event, Gemini lists a ladder of contracts at different
strike prices spaced $250 apart (e.g., $68,000 / $68,250 / $68,500 / ...).
There are typically 4 to 150 strikes per event (average ~30), covering a range
from deep out-of-the-money to deep in-the-money.

Events recur throughout the day and across days. The data spans:
  Earliest contract expiry: 2025-12-31
  Latest contract expiry:   2026-03-07

Total unique expiry events:  380 (759 were fetched; 380 are BTC price events,
                              the rest were duplicates across status filters)
Total individual contracts:  11,250


INSTRUMENT NAMING CONVENTION
-----------------------------
Format: GEMI-BTC{YYMMDDHHII}-HI{PRICE}

  GEMI    = Gemini exchange prefix
  BTC     = underlying asset
  YY      = 2-digit year
  MM      = 2-digit month
  DD      = 2-digit day
  HH      = 2-digit hour (UTC)
  II      = 2-digit minute (UTC)
  HI      = "higher than" (all contracts are of this type)
  PRICE   = strike price in USD (no commas or decimals)

Examples:
  GEMI-BTC2603030000-HI69000  -> BTC > $69,000 at 2026-03-03 00:00 UTC
  GEMI-BTC2603032200-HI68500  -> BTC > $68,500 at 2026-03-03 22:00 UTC

FILE DETAILS
------------

events.csv  (11,250 rows + header)
  One row per contract. Contains metadata snapshot at collection time.
  ~9,500 contracts had no market activity (deep OTM/ITM, zero liquidity).

  Columns:
    event_ticker      Unique identifier for the event
                      Format: BTC{YYMMDDHHII}
                      Example: BTC2603030000

    event_title       Human-readable event description
                      Example: "BTC price today at 7pm EST"

    event_status      Status of the parent event
                      Values: active, settled, approved

    event_created     ISO 8601 timestamp when the event was listed

    event_resolved    ISO 8601 timestamp when the event resolved (null if active)

    contract_id       Internal Gemini contract ID (format: {event_id}-{contract_id})

    contract_label    Human-readable strike description
                      Example: "BTC > $69,000"

    instrument        Full instrument symbol used in all other files
                      Example: GEMI-BTC2603030000-HI69000

    contract_status   Status of this specific contract
                      Values: active, settled, approved

    expiry            ISO 8601 timestamp when this contract expires/expired

    last_trade_price  Most recent trade price (0.01 to 0.99, or null)

    best_bid          Highest resting buy order at collection time (or null)

    best_ask          Lowest resting sell order at collection time (or null)

    total_shares      Total contracts outstanding (usually 0 in API response)


combined_candles.csv  (1,489,454 rows + header)
  5-minute OHLCV bars for all instruments that had any trading activity.
  Covers 1,333 unique instruments. Bars are sorted by instrument then time.
  Date range: 2026-02-23 to 2026-03-02.

  Columns:
    instrument    Instrument symbol (matches events.csv)
    timestamp_ms  Bar open time in Unix milliseconds (UTC)
    open          Price at bar open  (0.01 – 0.99)
    high          Highest price during bar
    low           Lowest price during bar
    close         Price at bar close
    volume        Number of contracts traded during bar (can be 0 between trades)

  Notes:
    - Prices are on a 0-1 scale representing probability (not USD)
    - Volume is in contracts, not USD
    - A bar with volume=0 is a "filler" bar emitted between real trades
    - Each instrument's candle history spans only its listing-to-expiry window
      (typically a few hours to a few days)
    - Candles per instrument range from 2 to 4,032 (median: 24, average: 1,117)


combined_trades.csv  (242,698 rows + header)
  Individual executed trades for all instruments that had activity.
  Covers 1,693 unique instruments. Sorted by instrument then timestamp.
  Date range: 2025-12-15 to 2026-03-02.

  Columns:
    instrument    Instrument symbol (matches events.csv)
    timestamp_ms  Trade execution time in Unix milliseconds (UTC)
    tid           Unique trade ID assigned by Gemini (integer)
    price         Executed price (0.01 – 0.99)
    amount        Number of contracts traded
    type          Aggressor side: "buy" or "sell"

  Notes:
    - "buy" means a buyer was the aggressor (lifted an ask)
    - "sell" means a seller was the aggressor (hit a bid)
    - Buy/sell split: 232,688 buys vs 10,010 sells (~96% buy-aggressed)
    - Amount range: 1 to 14,326 contracts per trade (average: 89)
    - The 500-trade limit per instrument means very active instruments
      may be truncated to only the most recent 500 trades


LIQUIDITY DISTRIBUTION
-----------------------
Of the 11,250 contracts, only ~1,300-1,700 had any market activity.
Liquid contracts are near-the-money: strikes within roughly $2,000-$3,000
of spot Bitcoin at the time of listing. Deep OTM and deep ITM contracts
are listed by Gemini but attract no trading interest.


SPOT BTC/USD PRICE DATA
-----------------------
To price these digital options and detect mispricings, spot BTC/USD data is
required. Two sources are provided, covering different periods and resolutions.

Gemini's candle API returns only a fixed rolling window with no historical
pagination, so coverage is limited for each timeframe. Binance.US supplements
the gap between the earliest prediction market trades and the start of Gemini's
fine-grained historical data.

data/gemini_spot/  (primary spot feed — Gemini's own settlement price)

  BTCUSD_1m.csv        1,440 rows | last ~24 hours     | 1-min bars
  BTCUSD_5m.csv        2,016 rows | last ~7 days        | 5-min bars
  BTCUSD_15m.csv       1,344 rows | last ~14 days       | 15-min bars
  BTCUSD_30m.csv       1,440 rows | last ~40 days       | 30-min bars
  BTCUSD_1hr.csv       1,416 rows | back to 2026-01-03  | 1-hr bars
  BTCUSD_6hr.csv         360 rows | back to 2025-12-03  | 6-hr bars
  BTCUSD_1day.csv        365 rows | back to 2025-03-03  | daily bars
  BTCUSD_composite.csv 5,705 rows | full range          | finest available per timestamp

  The composite file merges all timeframes, using the highest-resolution source
  for each timestamp. A "source_tf_min" column indicates the resolution in
  minutes (1 = 1m bar, 60 = 1hr bar, etc.).

  Columns (all files): timestamp_ms, datetime_utc, open, high, low, close, volume
  Prices are in USD. Volume is in BTC.

  These prices are sourced from Gemini's own BTC/USD market — the same price
  feed against which prediction market contracts settle.

data/gemini_spot/BTCUSD_binance_supplement_1m.csv
  (supplement — Binance.US BTCUSDT 1-minute bars)

  48,961 rows | 2025-12-01 to 2026-01-04 | 1-min bars
  Price range: $83,877 - $94,629

  Covers the period where Gemini's 1hr data is unavailable (before 2026-01-03),
  allowing fine-grained spot price lookup for the ~5 prediction market
  instruments with trades in December 2025.

  Note: Binance.US has ~59% zero-volume 1-minute bars due to thin US market
  liquidity. Prices in zero-volume bars are the last traded price carried
  forward — valid as a spot reference. The maximum observed gap between
  actual trades is 48 minutes. BTCUSDT (Tether) prices are used as a USD
  proxy; the USDT/USD spread is negligible for this purpose.

DERIBIT BTC OPTIONS (IV SURFACE FOR CALIBRATION)
-------------------------------------------------
Source: Deribit public API (no authentication required)
Collected: on-demand via src/data_collection/get_deribit_options.py
Location: data/deribit/

These are standard European BTC options (calls and puts) traded on Deribit.
Unlike Gemini's binary options, Deribit options pay max(S(T) - K, 0) or
max(K - S(T), 0) and are settled in BTC. Deribit is the dominant venue for
BTC options by open interest and provides the most liquid, benchmark IV surface
for BTC volatility.

This data is used for implied calibration of the Bates SVJ model. Because
Deribit options span many strikes and expiries simultaneously, their implied
volatility surface constrains all Bates model parameters (v0, kappa, theta,
sigma_v, rho, lambda, mu_j, sigma_j) in a single cross-sectional fit.

data/deribit/btc_options_chain.csv          (latest snapshot)
data/deribit/btc_options_chain_{stamp}.csv  (timestamped archive snapshots)

  ~1,024 rows per snapshot (512 calls + 512 puts)
  Expiries: daily for the nearest week, then weekly out ~1 month,
            monthly out to Dec 2026 (12 expiries total as of 2026-03-05)
  Most liquid expiry: the nearest Friday (monthly/quarterly expiries hold
                      the bulk of open interest)

  Columns:
    instrument_name   Deribit symbol, e.g. BTC-27MAR26-80000-C
    option_type       "call" or "put"
    strike            Strike price in USD
    expiry_ts_ms      Expiry timestamp (Unix ms, UTC)
    expiry_dt         ISO 8601 expiry string, e.g. 2026-03-27T08:00:00Z
    tte_years         Time to expiry in years at snapshot time
    underlying_price  Deribit forward price for this expiry in USD (F(T))
    mark_iv           Deribit implied volatility in % (annualised, Black-Scholes)
                      This is the primary signal for Bates calibration.
    mark_price_btc    Mark price in BTC (multiply by underlying_price for USD)
    bid_price_btc     Best bid in BTC
    ask_price_btc     Best ask in BTC
    mid_price_btc     Mid price in BTC
    open_interest     Open interest in contracts (1 contract = 1 BTC)
    volume_usd_24h    24-hour volume in USD
    snapshot_ts       UTC timestamp when this snapshot was taken

  Liquidity filter for calibration: open_interest > 10 BTC
  Moneyness filter:                  0.75 < K / F(T) < 1.35
  Expiry filter:                     tte_years > 3/365 (at least 3 days)

  Note: mark_price_btc prices are denominated in BTC. To convert to USD:
    price_usd = mark_price_btc * underlying_price
  The underlying_price is the forward price, which already incorporates the
  cost of carry (BTC has no dividend, so F ≈ S * e^{rT}).

CALIBRATION OUTPUT
------------------
  data/deribit/bates_params_implied.json
    BatesParams JSON produced by src/calibration/implied.py.
    Represents the risk-neutral (Q-measure) Bates model parameters that
    best fit the Deribit IV surface at the time of the last calibration run.
